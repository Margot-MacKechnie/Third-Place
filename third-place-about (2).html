About Third Place

Building community, one connection at a time

Why "Third Place"?

The term "third place" was coined by sociologist Ray Oldenburg to describe the social spaces outside of home (first place) and work (second place) where community naturally forms. These are the coffee shops, bookstores, pubs, and parks where people gather not out of obligation, but by choice—where strangers become neighbors and neighbors become friends.

"Third places are the living rooms of our society—neutral ground where people can gather, unwind, and form the connections that make life worth living."

The Problem We're Solving

We're facing a loneliness epidemic. Studies show that meaningful social connections have declined dramatically over the past few decades, with serious consequences for our mental and physical health. Traditional gathering spaces have disappeared, replaced by transactional environments that prioritize speed over connection.

Our Approach

Third Place addresses this need by creating an environment specifically designed for human connection. By combining a bookstore, café, and plant shop, we give people multiple reasons to visit and multiple ways to engage—whether they're browsing books, working on their laptop, attending a workshop, or simply enjoying a quiet moment with a plant.

We're inspired by the Nordic model of social infrastructure—the communal libraries, public saunas, and neighborhood gathering spaces that contribute to consistently high happiness rankings in countries like Denmark and Finland. These aren't just amenities; they're investments in social capital that pay dividends in community wellbeing.

The Market Opportunity

While our mission is social, our business model is sound. Independent bookstores have seen a resurgence in recent years, with over 300 new stores opening since 2009. The specialty coffee market continues to grow at 8% annually. And houseplant sales exploded during the pandemic, with the market now valued at over $1.7 billion.

More importantly, people are actively seeking meaningful experiences over material goods—they want spaces that offer more than just products. They want community.

What's Next

We're currently in the planning phase, conducting customer discovery and refining our business model. Our target launch location is Madison, Wisconsin—a city with a strong independent business culture, walkable neighborhoods, and a community that values sustainability and connection.

This is more than a business plan. It's a vision for how we can rebuild the social fabric of our communities, one conversation at a time.

What is a Third Place?

"Third places are the informal public gathering spaces that host regular, voluntary, and happily anticipated gatherings of individuals beyond the realms of home and work."
— Ray Oldenburg, Sociologist

Third places are essential community anchors where people connect, belong, and build relationships naturally. They're the coffee shops where neighbors become friends, the bookstores where conversations spark, and the gardens where communities grow. Our space brings together books, coffee, and plants to create the kind of welcoming environment where everyone feels at home.

Three Spaces, One Community

Curated Bookstore

Independent, local, and thoughtfully selected titles spanning fiction, non-fiction, and special interest. A space for discovery and slow browsing.

Artisan Café

Locally roasted coffee, house-made pastries, and seasonal menu items. A welcoming atmosphere that encourages lingering and conversation.

Plant Shop

Indoor plants, succulents, and seasonal flowers. Educational workshops on plant care and sustainable living practices.

Our Mission

Third Place exists to create authentic community connections in an era of increasing social isolation. We believe that people need accessible, welcoming spaces beyond home and work—places where friendships form naturally, conversations flow freely, and everyone belongs. By combining books, coffee, and plants, we're building more than a business; we're cultivating community.

Meet the Founder

Margot MacKechnie is a senior at Syracuse University's Whitman School of Management, studying Entrepreneurship and Emerging Enterprises with a minor in Information Technology, Design, and Startups.

With technical skills in Python, Java, and JavaScript, plus consulting experience through WISE Women's Business Center, Margot combines business strategy with sustainability principles to create ventures that serve both profit and purpose.

Third Place represents her vision for addressing social isolation through intentional design—creating spaces where community naturally flourishes.
